Our first assignment for "Interactive Media: Web" was to create a typeface entirely out of HTML and CSS. Building off the examples done in class, my significant other and I developed this monospace typeface.

I've always had an appreciation for the aesthetic of command line/terminal which is my reasoning for going monospace.

We also were suppose to design a sort of 'Web Poster' for one of a few choices of conferences. I ended choosing Theorizing The Web mainly due to my broad interest in computers. The previous week I was unfortunately away, but the class was introduced to CSS grids. I actually never knew about this grid function with divs and I really, really love it now that I realize it exists. Most of the time spent designer the poster was time spent just playing around with CSS grids.

Click here for the Web Poster

One of the requirements was that a CSS typeface must be used. I choose to implement my own typeface as a sort 'type as image' background sort of element. My reasoning for this is purely technical. I had originally designed the typeface to be about 500px in length and 300-500px in width. This would prove too large for the template provided so my girlfriend and I ultimately scaled everything down to half size. While scaling, we already ran into issues where numbers were not divisible by two. This resulted in some less than desired, but still managable results. Going into the poster, the typeface was still to large to print long words with. I really did not want to scale it down again due to more odd numbers I'd have to divide unevenly. Thus, I simply improvised.
