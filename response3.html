In response to 'The Poetry of Tools' by Laurel Schwulst

Why does Laurel Schwulst (Links to an external site.) advocate for individual websites over social media? Do you agree with her? Please explain your answer.

Laurel Schwulst’s reasoning for advocating individuals owning their own websites over their own social media profiles stems from the corporatization of the web. She believes in this day and age, “we need individuals rather than corporations to guide the web’s future”. Rather than working to profit off people (through invasive advertising, data mining, etc) the web should be geared towards empowering the individual. The web in itself is a very powerful tool which allows anyone to create and share essentially anything. Schwult’s herself spends a majority of her essay explaining how a websites can take an unlimited amount of forms. In essence, the web allows us to manifest any world our imaginations can conjure up and share them on a global scale. While corporations may make it easier for us by bridging the technical difficulties, we as people can’t let our collective voices, the ones that shape the web, be shaped by corporations.

2) In this article, Schwulst outlines several metaphors defining the behaviour of websites. Write a paragraph describing your own metaphor for a website. Put that text into its own simple website designed to extend that comparison. Upload it to your github server and post the link here.

To me, a website is simply a space. As with any space, it is how the owner/user/webmaster/whatever defines this space which gives it meaning or purpose. What makes this space special however is its separation from our own reality. In a way, this space exists in a plane made up entirely of electrical signals. These signals are then abstracted into instructions our computer can request and perform. It’s this sense of abstraction and detachment from our own reality which allows us to shape this said space’s form and function. In that sense, it is a space nearly limitless potential.
